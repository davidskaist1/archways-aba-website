Frequently Asked Questions

Applied Behavior Analysis (ABA) is a scientifically validated approach that uses principles of learning theory to improve socially significant behaviors. For children with autism, ABA therapy focuses on developing communication, social, self-care, academic, and adaptive skills while reducing challenging behaviors. Programs are individualized and based on each child's unique assessment.

Most major insurance plans and Missouri Medicaid cover ABA therapy for children diagnosed with Autism Spectrum Disorder. We accept most insurance providers including Medicaid, Aetna, Blue Cross Blue Shield, Cigna, Humana, United Healthcare, and Tricare. We provide free benefits verification — contact us and we'll check your coverage at no cost.

The recommended number of hours varies based on your child's age, diagnosis, and individual goals. After a comprehensive assessment, our BCBA will recommend an appropriate level of care — typically ranging from 10 to 40 hours per week. We work collaboratively with families and insurance providers to find the right fit.
