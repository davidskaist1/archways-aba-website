Does Missouri Medicaid Cover ABA Therapy? Everything You Need to Know

The short answer is yes — and Archways ABA handles the entire insurance process for your family. Here's a plain-language breakdown of what's covered and how to access it.

March 3, 2025 11 min read By Archways ABA Clinical Team

For many Missouri families, cost is the first — and sometimes only — barrier standing between their child and the ABA therapy they need. The good news: if your child is enrolled in Missouri Medicaid (officially called MO HealthNet), ABA therapy is a covered benefit. And at Archways ABA, we handle the entire insurance and authorization process so you can focus on your child — not paperwork.

This article explains everything you need to know about Missouri Medicaid coverage for ABA therapy, including eligibility, what's covered, how prior authorization works, and what to do if you face obstacles.

Yes — Missouri Medicaid Covers ABA Therapy

Missouri's Medicaid program, MO HealthNet, covers Applied Behavior Analysis (ABA) therapy for children diagnosed with autism spectrum disorder (ASD). This coverage applies to children enrolled in traditional MO HealthNet as well as children covered through managed care plans like Home State Health, Healthy Blue, and United Healthcare Community Plan.

This is significant: ABA therapy can cost $40,000–$60,000 per year without insurance, placing it out of reach for most families. Medicaid coverage removes that financial barrier for eligible Missouri families.

Eligibility Requirements for Medicaid-Funded ABA in Missouri

To access ABA therapy through MO HealthNet, your child generally needs to meet the following criteria:

  • Autism spectrum disorder diagnosis — A formal ASD diagnosis from a licensed psychologist, developmental pediatrician, or psychiatrist
  • Age — Coverage is available for children through age 20 in most MO HealthNet plans
  • Active MO HealthNet enrollment — Your child must be actively enrolled in Missouri Medicaid
  • Medical necessity determination — A BCBA conducts an assessment showing ABA therapy is medically necessary for your child

At Archways ABA, we handle the medical necessity documentation as part of our intake process — you don't need to figure this out on your own.

What Does Missouri Medicaid Cover for ABA?

Missouri Medicaid covers a comprehensive range of ABA services, including:

  • Initial assessment — The comprehensive skills assessment and functional behavior assessment conducted by a BCBA to develop your child's treatment plan
  • Direct therapy hours — Sessions with a trained RBT under BCBA supervision, in your home, school, or clinic
  • BCBA supervision — Regular oversight sessions where the BCBA reviews data, observes therapy, and updates the treatment plan
  • Parent training — Sessions teaching parents to implement ABA strategies at home
  • Re-assessments — Periodic reassessments to update treatment goals

Coverage limits (number of hours per week or year) vary by plan and by the individual child's medical necessity determination. Your BCBA will recommend the appropriate therapy intensity and document the justification.

Understanding Prior Authorization

Before ABA therapy can begin, your insurance plan requires prior authorization (PA) — an approval process where the insurer reviews clinical documentation and approves a specific number of therapy hours for a defined period (usually 6 months to 1 year).

The prior authorization process typically involves:

  1. Archways ABA's BCBA conducts an initial assessment and develops a treatment plan
  2. We submit a prior authorization request to your child's MO HealthNet plan with clinical documentation
  3. The plan reviews the request (typically 5–14 business days)
  4. Once approved, therapy begins
  5. Before each authorization period ends, we submit a renewal request with updated progress data

At Archways ABA, we manage the entire prior authorization process. You will never have to navigate insurance paperwork alone.

Missouri Medicaid Managed Care Plans and ABA

Most Missouri Medicaid recipients are enrolled in a managed care organization (MCO) rather than traditional fee-for-service Medicaid. The main MCOs in Missouri that cover ABA therapy include:

  • Home State Health
  • Healthy Blue (Anthem)
  • United Healthcare Community Plan
  • Missouri Care

Archways ABA is in-network with Missouri Medicaid and its managed care plans. Coverage details, copay amounts, and authorization processes vary slightly by plan — our team is experienced with all of them and will walk you through your specific coverage.

Private Insurance vs. Medicaid: What's the Difference?

If your child has both Medicaid and private insurance (sometimes called "dual coverage"), Medicaid typically acts as the secondary payer — meaning private insurance is billed first, and Medicaid may cover remaining costs. Our billing team handles coordination of benefits so you don't have to.

Missouri also has an autism insurance mandate (RSMo 376.1224) requiring most private insurance plans to cover ABA therapy. If you have private insurance without Medicaid, you likely have ABA coverage as well — call us and we'll verify.

What If My Authorization Request Is Denied?

Denials happen, but they are not the end of the road. If your child's ABA authorization is denied, you have the right to appeal. Common reasons for denials include incomplete documentation, plan errors, or incorrect diagnosis codes — many of which can be corrected on appeal.

At Archways ABA, we assist families with the appeals process and can provide additional clinical documentation to support the appeal. We've successfully reversed many initial denials.
