What It Is

A Dedicated Clinic Built for
Intensive ABA Programming

Center-based ABA therapy takes place in our purpose-built clinic facilities, where children work with their Registered Behavior Technician (RBT) in individual therapy rooms specifically designed to support focused learning. Each child's program is developed and overseen by an on-site Board Certified Behavior Analyst (BCBA).

The clinic environment offers something unique: a consistent, controlled learning space with specialized materials, immediate access to supervisory staff, peer interaction opportunities, and the ability to deliver high-intensity programming across a full day. For children who need more hours or benefit from separation from home-environment distractions, center-based therapy is often the most effective choice.

Clinical Advantage

Center-based ABA allows BCBAs to directly observe sessions, immediately consult on programming, and conduct real-time training for RBTs — resulting in higher fidelity to your child's individualized treatment plan.

Key Benefits of Center-Based ABA

  • Structured Learning Environment — Dedicated therapy rooms minimize distractions and support focused, productive sessions.
  • Peer Interaction Opportunities — Children practice social and communication skills with other children in a supervised, therapeutic setting.
  • Intensive Programming — Center-based settings support higher therapy hours per week, often 25–40+ hours for children who need intensive intervention.
  • Full Supervision Team On-Site — Your BCBA is present throughout the day — not just for a supervision visit — allowing immediate program adjustments.
  • Specialized Materials and Spaces — Clinics are stocked with dedicated ABA teaching materials, sensory rooms, and group activity spaces unavailable in most homes.
  • Consistent Routine-Based Learning — Arrival routines, mealtimes, and transitions are all built into the program, teaching real-world skills in a consistent, predictable structure.
What to Expect

What a Typical Day at the Clinic Looks Like

From arrival to pickup, every moment is a learning opportunity. Here's how a center-based day is structured.

Arrival Routine

Each morning begins with a structured arrival routine — hanging up belongings, greeting staff, checking schedules. These transitions are rich teaching moments for independence and communication skills.

1:1 Therapy Blocks

The core of the day is dedicated 1:1 time with your child's RBT, working on individualized skill targets across communication, social, academic, and daily living domains — with data collected on every trial.

Group Activities

Structured group time allows children to practice skills with peers — turn-taking, cooperative play, conversation, and following group instructions — in a supervised, therapeutically planned setting.

Peer Play & Social Skills

Naturalistic play opportunities are woven into the schedule, allowing children to generalize learned social and communication skills in less structured peer interactions throughout the day.

Meals & Snacks as Teaching

Mealtimes are not just nutrition breaks — they're opportunities to practice requesting, waiting, table manners, and mealtime communication in a structured, supported environment.

Daily Parent Update at Pickup

At the end of each session, families receive a brief update on the day's progress, goals worked on, and any notable events — keeping you informed and connected every single day.

Who It's For

Is Center-Based ABA Right for Your Child?

Center-based ABA therapy is particularly effective for children who:

  • Need high-intensity hours (25+ per week)
  • Thrive with structure and consistent routines
  • Benefit from peer modeling and interaction
  • Have distracting home environments
  • Are preparing for a school classroom setting
  • Need early intensive behavioral intervention (EIBI)
  • Require on-site BCBA presence throughout the day

Many children benefit from a combination of center-based and in-home services. Our clinical team will recommend the best model for your child during your free intake assessment.
